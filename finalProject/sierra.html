The Sierra Region

Between majestic mountains, verdant valleys, and towns steeped in history, the Peruvian Sierra offers an experience that combines tradition, hospitality, and ancestral flavors. Walking through its cobblestone streets is like discovering a Peru that preserves its living soul, where each dish tells a story passed down from generation to generation. From the fresh air of Cusco to the festivals of Huancayo, the mountains invite you to lose yourself in its landscapes and let yourself be won over by its cuisine.

Flavors of the Peruvian Sierra

Pachamanca

An Andean gem prepared underground with hot stones, where meats, potatoes, and herbs blend to create a smoky and unmistakable flavor.

A cold, aromatic, and creamy dessert with cinnamon and coconut, perfect for cooling off at altitude.

Cuy Frito

Fried guinea pig is a culinary gem of the Peruvian Andes. With its crispy skin and tender meat, it's enjoyed alongside golden potatoes, toasted corn, and Creole salad, reflecting the authentic flavor of the Peruvian highlands.

Quinoa Chupe

A thick and nutritious soup that combines quinoa with milk, cheese, and potatoes, a symbol of highland comfort food.

Head Soup

A traditional Sunday breakfast in many highland towns, this hearty broth is made with cow's head, potatoes, and local herbs. Each sip revitalizes the body and spirit, reflecting the warmth and strength of Andean tradition.

Humitas

Tender ground corn, mixed with cheese or sugar, then wrapped in husks and steamed. A comforting Andean recipe that celebrates the simplicity and richness of ancestral cooking.
