The Coast Region

The Peruvian coast is characterized by its incredible culinary diversity, with dishes that combine seafood and desert produce. Highlights include ceviche, seafood rice, lomo saltado, and many more. But the coast doesn't just captivate with its flavor — it also enchants with its landscapes and warm climate. From the beaches of Máncora to the cliffs of Lima, every corner invites you to enjoy the sea, the breeze, and good food. Its coastal towns are brimming with life, music, and tradition, reflecting the joyful spirit of modern Peru.

Flavors of the Coast

Ceviche

An iconic Peruvian dish made with fresh fish marinated in lemon juice, onion, aji, and cilantro. It's refreshing, tangy, and full of seafood flavor.

Anticucho

Meat skewers (traditionally beef heart) marinated in panca chili, vinegar, and spices, grilled and served with potatoes and corn. Very popular on Peruvian streets.

Causa Limeña

Layers of mashed yellow potatoes mixed with lemon and yellow chili, filled with chicken, tuna, or seafood. Served cold, garnished with avocado and hard-boiled egg.

Lomo Saltado

Sautéed beef with onion, tomato, and French fries, accompanied by white rice. It combines Chinese techniques with Peruvian ingredients—pure Creole flavor.

Arroz con Mariscos

Rice cooked with a mixture of seafood such as squid, shrimp, and clams, seasoned with chili and herbs. It's colorful, aromatic, and very nutritious.

Seco

A thick stew made with meat (beef, lamb, or chicken) cooked with cilantro, chicha de jora, and spices. Served with white rice and beans—ideal for those who love intense flavors.
