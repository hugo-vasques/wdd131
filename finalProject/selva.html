The Jungle Region

The Peruvian jungle is pure life: sounds, aromas, and colors blend in an explosion of nature. Amid winding rivers, welcoming towns, and unique biodiversity, visitors are immersed in an exotic and authentic experience. Its cuisine is equally vibrant: ingredients that only grow in the Amazon, intense flavors, and combinations that awaken all the senses. Each dish is an adventure that reflects the connection between humans and the jungle.

Flavors of the Peruvian Jungle

Juane

The Amazonian emblem: rice, egg, and chicken wrapped in bijao leaves, seasoned with native spices and steamed to perfection. Traditionally enjoyed during the San Juan festival, it symbolizes celebration, tradition, and the spirit of the Peruvian jungle.

Tacacho con Cecina

Roasted and mashed plantain mixed with lard, served with slices of smoky, cured pork. This rustic and hearty dish reflects the warmth of the Amazonian kitchen and the balance between simplicity and deep flavor.

Inchicapi de Gallina

A creamy soup made with peanuts, cassava, chicken, and aromatic herbs. Nourishing and full of tradition, this comforting dish captures the soul of the jungle — rich, earthy, and made to be shared.

Patarashca

Fresh river fish seasoned with herbs, wrapped in bijao leaves, and roasted over an open fire. The result is a tender, aromatic delicacy that brings out the authentic taste and essence of the Amazon rainforest.

Masato

A traditional fermented cassava drink, naturally sweet and mildly tangy. Beyond its flavor, it's a drink of hospitality — shared with visitors as a gesture of friendship and unity in Amazonian communities.

Chonta Salad

Made with the heart of palm, light, crunchy, and refreshing. Drizzled with citrus and a touch of chili, it perfectly balances the intense, smoky flavors of the jungle's most beloved dishes.
